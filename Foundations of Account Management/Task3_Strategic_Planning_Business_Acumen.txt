DELIVERABLE 1: RESOURCE + BUDGET ALLOCATION SUMMARY (90 Days)
----------------------------------------------------------------

Over the first 90 days, my time allocation will shift intentionally across
three phases. Days 1–30 are relationship-heavy: roughly 50% of my weekly
account hours go toward discovery calls, onboarding documentation, and
establishing communication cadence. Days 31–60 shift toward execution —
approximately 35% of time moves into coordinating internal resources such
as the technical support team and solutions consultants, while 40% remains
on direct client communication. Days 61–90 prioritize review and strategic
alignment, with increased time on reporting, performance analysis, and
planning the next quarter. One key budgeting consideration shaping this
allocation is the cost of internal specialist hours: by front-loading
discovery and clearly scoping Precision Marketing Solutions' needs in
Month 1, I reduce the risk of unplanned specialist involvement in Months 2
and 3, keeping support costs predictable and within the account's allocated
service budget.

DELIVERABLE 2: PERFORMANCE METRICS (90-Day Tracking Plan)
----------------------------------------------------------------

1. CUSTOMER SATISFACTION SCORE (CSAT)
   What it measures: Marcus and his team's satisfaction with communication,
   responsiveness, and overall account management quality.
   How tracked: Brief pulse surveys sent after each major touchpoint or
   milestone, targeting a score of 85% or higher throughout the 90 days.
   Why it matters: CSAT is the clearest direct indicator of whether the
   client relationship is being managed effectively and whether our service
   delivery is meeting expectations.

2. RESPONSE TIME TO CLIENT INQUIRIES
   What it measures: The time elapsed between a client inquiry or request
   and Sarah's first substantive response.
   How tracked: Logged in the CRM with timestamps on all inbound and
   outbound communications; target is a response within 4 business hours.
   Why it matters: Response time is a visible, daily reflection of
   reliability. Consistent adherence signals professionalism and directly
   reinforces Marcus's expectation of dependable follow-through.

3. ONBOARDING MILESTONE COMPLETION RATE
   What it measures: The percentage of planned onboarding tasks completed
   on schedule across the 30-, 60-, and 90-day checkpoints.
   How tracked: A shared milestone tracker updated weekly, reviewed
   formally at each end-of-phase check-in with Marcus.
   Why it matters: Milestone adherence demonstrates organized execution
   and gives both the client and the director a clear, objective view of
   account progress beyond qualitative impressions.

4. INTERNAL SPECIALIST HOURS USED VS. BUDGETED
   What it measures: The number of hours drawn from technical support,
   solutions consultants, or other internal resources compared to the
   projected budget for the account.
   How tracked: Logged in the internal resource management system and
   reviewed biweekly by Sarah and the director.
   Why it matters: Tracking specialist hours against the budget ensures
   the account remains financially sustainable. It also creates an early
   warning system if scope is expanding beyond original projections,
   allowing for proactive conversation with Marcus before costs escalate.

5. ISSUE RESOLUTION TIMELINE
   What it measures: The average number of business days between a client-
   reported issue and its confirmed resolution.
   How tracked: Every issue is logged in the CRM at intake with a target
   resolution date; closure is documented and reviewed in monthly reports.
   Why it matters: How quickly and completely problems are resolved is one
   of the strongest predictors of client retention. Tracking this metric
   holds the internal team accountable and surfaces patterns that can be
   addressed systematically rather than reactively.

6. ACTION ITEM FOLLOW-THROUGH RATE
   What it measures: The percentage of committed action items from meetings
   and calls that are completed by their agreed-upon deadlines.
   How tracked: The running action-item tracker is reviewed at every
   touchpoint; incomplete items are flagged and carried forward with an
   updated owner and deadline.
   Why it matters: This metric directly measures reliability at the
   operational level. A high follow-through rate builds the kind of
   demonstrated accountability that earns long-term client confidence —
   particularly for a client like Marcus, who has expressed that dependable
   follow-through is a core expectation.

DELIVERABLE 3: STRATEGIC REFLECTION
----------------------------------------------------------------

The allocations and metrics in this plan were selected with two priorities
in mind: protecting the client relationship in the short term and building
a scalable, efficient account structure for the long term. Concentrating
relationship-building effort in Days 1–30 is not simply a courtesy — it is
a strategic investment. The clearer I understand Precision Marketing
Solutions' priorities early, the less reactive and more proactive the
account management becomes in Months 2 and 3. The metrics chosen reflect
that same philosophy: each one is measurable, actionable, and tied directly
to a business outcome rather than activity for its own sake. CSAT and
follow-through rate measure trust; response time and resolution timelines
measure reliability; milestone completion and specialist hours measure
efficiency and fiscal responsibility. Together, they give both Marcus and
my director a complete, honest picture of account health at any point in
the 90 days.
