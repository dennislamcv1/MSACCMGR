DELIVERABLE 1: ETHICAL DECISION-MAKING FRAMEWORK
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Effective ethical decision-making in enterprise account management requires a
structured, repeatable process that can be applied consistently regardless of
geography, stakeholder profile, or deal complexity.

TechFlow's framework follows five sequential steps. First, IDENTIFY the
dilemma clearly -- distinguish ethical concerns from commercial disagreements
and name the specific values, regulations, or obligations at risk. Second,
GATHER CONTEXT by consulting relevant policy, legal guidance, and subject-matter
experts before acting. Third, EVALUATE OPTIONS by weighing each course of action
against four lenses: legal compliance, stakeholder impact, organizational
values, and long-term trust. Fourth, DECIDE AND DOCUMENT by selecting the
option that best satisfies all four lenses and recording the reasoning in
writing. Fifth, REVIEW AND LEARN by closing each resolved dilemma with a
retrospective to strengthen future decision quality.

This framework is designed to be consultative rather than reactive, ensuring
that ethical judgment is embedded into account management practice -- not
applied only in moments of crisis.

DELIVERABLE 2: AI PRIVACY & HUMAN OVERSIGHT PROTOCOLS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1. DATA CLASSIFICATION BEFORE AI TOOL USE
   Before inputting any information into an AI tool -- including Microsoft
   Copilot, generative AI assistants, or automated analytics platforms --
   account team members must classify the data according to TechFlow's
   four-tier schema: Public, Internal, Confidential, and Restricted.
   Only Public and Internal data may be processed through AI tools operating
   under standard enterprise licensing. Confidential or Restricted data
   (including GlobalCorp's financial records, strategic plans, personnel
   information, or proprietary architecture details) may only be processed
   through AI tools covered by a validated Data Processing Agreement (DPA)
   and approved by TechFlow's Information Security team. No exceptions are
   permitted, regardless of timeline pressure or perceived low risk.

--------

2. PROHIBITION ON PII INPUT INTO NON-COMPLIANT AI SYSTEMS
   Personally Identifiable Information (PII) relating to GlobalCorp
   employees, end users, or customers must never be entered into AI tools
   that have not been assessed and approved for PII processing under
   applicable privacy law (GDPR, CCPA, APPI, or equivalent). This includes
   names paired with job titles, email addresses, device identifiers, or any
   combination of data points that could identify an individual. Account
   managers who are uncertain whether a dataset contains PII must consult
   TechFlow's Privacy Officer before proceeding -- the default assumption
   is that sensitivity exists until confirmed otherwise.

--------

3. MANDATORY HUMAN REVIEW OF AI-GENERATED OUTPUTS
   All content generated by AI tools that is intended for external use --
   including client proposals, executive briefings, contract summaries,
   financial models, compliance documentation, and presentation materials --
   must undergo human review and approval before distribution. Review
   responsibility is assigned by content type:
     - Legal or contractual content: Legal Counsel review required
     - Financial projections: Finance Business Partner sign-off required
     - Client-facing proposals: Senior Account Manager approval required
     - Regulatory compliance statements: Compliance Officer confirmation required
   AI-generated content may not be sent to GlobalCorp or any external party
   without at least one layer of qualified human review on record. "Generated
   with AI assistance" must be logged in the internal document version history.

--------

4. PROHIBITION ON AI-GENERATED COMMITMENTS
   AI tools may not be used to generate, communicate, or imply contractual
   commitments, pricing approvals, SLA guarantees, or delivery timelines on
   TechFlow's behalf -- whether directly to GlobalCorp or in internal
   documents that may be shared. All formal commitments must originate from
   authorized personnel following TechFlow's standard approval hierarchy.
   This protocol addresses the risk of AI-produced language that sounds
   authoritative but lacks the internal validation required for enforceability
   and accuracy.

--------

5. TRANSPARENCY WITH CLIENTS REGARDING AI USAGE
   If GlobalCorp stakeholders ask whether AI tools were used in the
   preparation of proposals, analyses, or other deliverables, TechFlow
   account team members will respond honestly and describe our AI usage
   guidelines in plain terms. We will not misrepresent AI-assisted work
   as entirely human-generated, nor will we overstate AI capabilities in
   ways that inflate expectations of our delivery model. Transparency about
   AI usage builds the kind of trust that sustains multi-year partnerships.

--------

6. CONTINUOUS AI GOVERNANCE REVIEW
   TechFlow's AI governance protocols will be reviewed no less than quarterly
   by the Information Security, Legal, and Compliance functions -- and
   immediately following any material change in applicable regulation (e.g.,
   EU AI Act implementation updates, U.S. federal AI guidance, or new GDPR
   enforcement decisions). Account teams will be notified of policy changes
   within five business days and will receive updated training materials.
   Given the pace of AI development, standing protocols must be treated as
   living documents, not fixed policy.

DELIVERABLE 3: REGULATORY COMPLIANCE CHECKLIST
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1. GENERAL DATA PROTECTION REGULATION (GDPR) -- EUROPEAN UNION
   Applicability: GlobalCorp's EU headquarters (Frankfurt) and operations
                  across member states make GDPR the most immediately
                  material privacy regulation in this engagement.
   Required Actions:
   [ ] Confirm lawful basis for processing any personal data shared between
       TechFlow and GlobalCorp under the partnership (Article 6)
   [ ] Establish a Data Processing Agreement (DPA) governing TechFlow's
       role as a data processor (Article 28)
   [ ] Confirm data residency requirements -- EU personal data must remain
       within EU-approved jurisdictions unless Standard Contractual Clauses
       (SCCs) or equivalent transfer mechanisms are in place
   [ ] Document and agree on data breach notification timelines
       (72-hour supervisory authority notification per Article 33)
   [ ] Assign a TechFlow point of contact for GDPR-related inquiries
       from GlobalCorp's Data Protection Officer (DPO)

--------

2. ACT ON THE PROTECTION OF PERSONAL INFORMATION (APPI) -- JAPAN
   Applicability: GlobalCorp's APAC operations led by the Tokyo regional
                  team trigger obligations under Japan's APPI, particularly
                  for cross-border data transfers.
   Required Actions:
   [ ] Assess whether GlobalCorp's Japanese operations will transfer personal
       data to TechFlow systems outside Japan, and if so, confirm compliance
       mechanism (consent or equivalent protection standard)
   [ ] Ensure TechFlow's data handling practices for APAC data meet APPI's
       third-party provision restrictions
   [ ] Confirm that any data shared during the engagement is processed for
       the specific purpose disclosed to the original data subjects

--------

3. FOREIGN CORRUPT PRACTICES ACT (FCPA) & UK BRIBERY ACT
   Applicability: TechFlow is a U.S.-headquartered company conducting
                  business with a multi-national client -- both statutes
                  apply extraterritorially to TechFlow's conduct.
   Required Actions:
   [ ] Brief all account team members on anti-bribery obligations before
       any in-person GlobalCorp engagement, particularly in jurisdictions
       with elevated corruption-risk ratings
   [ ] Document and obtain pre-approval for any client entertainment,
       gifts, or hospitality expenses above $75 USD equivalent per
       individual per occasion
   [ ] Screen any third-party agents, consultants, or referral partners
       involved in the GlobalCorp engagement against FCPA compliance
       requirements prior to engagement
   [ ] Maintain complete records of all payments, gifts, and business
       courtesies extended to GlobalCorp stakeholders throughout the
       partnership lifecycle

--------

4. EU AI ACT COMPLIANCE CONSIDERATIONS
   Applicability: To the extent TechFlow's platform incorporates AI-driven
                  capabilities deployed within GlobalCorp's EU operations,
                  the EU AI Act's risk classification framework applies.
   Required Actions:
   [ ] Classify TechFlow's AI-enabled product features under the EU AI Act's
       risk tiers (minimal, limited, high, or unacceptable risk)
   [ ] For any features classified as high-risk, prepare required technical
       documentation including transparency, human oversight, and accuracy
       standards
   [ ] Confirm that GlobalCorp's EU team receives clear disclosure of any
       AI-driven decision-making features embedded in TechFlow's platform
   [ ] Monitor EU AI Act implementation timelines and adjust product
       disclosures and contractual terms accordingly as provisions take
       effect between 2024 and 2027

--------

5. CALIFORNIA CONSUMER PRIVACY ACT (CCPA) / CPRA
   Applicability: If GlobalCorp's California-based employees or customers
                  interact with TechFlow's platform, CCPA/CPRA obligations
                  are triggered for California residents' data.
   Required Actions:
   [ ] Confirm whether California residents' personal information will be
       processed by TechFlow under the partnership
   [ ] Ensure TechFlow's data processing activities qualify under the
       business-to-business (B2B) exemption where applicable
   [ ] Include appropriate CCPA data subject rights provisions in the
       contractual framework (right to know, delete, opt-out of sale)

--------

6. EXPORT CONTROLS & CROSS-BORDER TECHNOLOGY TRANSFER
   Applicability: TechFlow's enterprise software platform may be subject
                  to U.S. Export Administration Regulations (EAR) for
                  deployments in certain jurisdictions, particularly if
                  encryption technology is involved.
   Required Actions:
   [ ] Conduct an export classification review of TechFlow's platform
       components before any cross-border deployment within GlobalCorp's
       international sites
   [ ] Screen GlobalCorp's international locations against U.S. restricted
       party lists and embargoed jurisdiction lists prior to deployment
   [ ] Obtain any required export licenses or confirm applicable license
       exceptions before technology transfer proceeds
   [ ] Document export compliance review in the master deal file

DELIVERABLE 4: ETHICAL CONFLICT RESOLUTION PROCEDURES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1. EARLY IDENTIFICATION & INTERNAL ESCALATION
   The moment an account team member identifies a potential ethical conflict --
   whether involving a client request, an internal instruction, a partner
   behavior, or a regulatory gray area -- it must be documented in writing
   and escalated to the Senior Account Manager within 24 hours. Delay in
   escalation is itself a compliance risk. The Senior Account Manager then
   has 48 hours to determine whether the conflict can be resolved at the
   account team level or requires escalation to Legal, Compliance, or
   executive leadership. All escalations are logged in TechFlow's Ethics
   Case Register with a unique reference number, resolution owner, and
   target resolution date.

--------

2. STRUCTURED STAKEHOLDER DIALOGUE
   Once an ethical conflict is identified and logged, the resolution process
   prioritizes direct, good-faith dialogue with the relevant stakeholder --
   whether internal or external -- before any formal or adversarial process
   is invoked. For conflicts involving GlobalCorp, the Senior Account Manager
   will request a dedicated conversation with the appropriate counterpart
   (not embedded within a commercial negotiation meeting) to name the concern
   clearly, explain TechFlow's position and the underlying policy or
   regulatory basis, and invite a collaborative resolution. This dialogue
   is documented with agreed next steps and a resolution timeline.

--------

3. INDEPENDENT REVIEW FOR UNRESOLVED CONFLICTS
   If direct stakeholder dialogue does not produce a resolution within the
   agreed timeline, or if the conflict involves a matter of legal exposure,
   financial materiality, or potential regulatory violation, the case is
   referred to TechFlow's Ethics Review Committee -- a standing body
   comprising the General Counsel, Chief Compliance Officer, and a senior
   business leader independent of the affected account. The Committee
   reviews all available documentation and renders a written determination
   within five business days. The determination is binding on the account
   team and communicated to relevant parties in writing.

--------

4. CLIENT AND PARTNER COMMUNICATION STANDARDS
   When communicating about ethical conflicts with GlobalCorp or third-party
   partners, TechFlow account team members will:
     - Be factual and non-accusatory in describing the concern
     - Reference the specific policy, regulation, or value at issue rather
       than characterizing individual conduct
     - Avoid communicating under time pressure or in informal settings
       (email or written communication is preferred over verbal-only)
     - Never make concessions on ethical matters as a substitute for
       commercial concessions (e.g., waiving a compliance requirement in
       exchange for pricing flexibility)
   This communication standard preserves the relationship while maintaining
   TechFlow's integrity position clearly and professionally.

--------

5. POST-RESOLUTION REVIEW & SYSTEMIC IMPROVEMENT
   Following the close of any ethical conflict -- regardless of how it was
   resolved -- the Senior Account Manager completes a structured Post-
   Resolution Review within 10 business days. This review addresses:
     (a) Root cause: What conditions allowed the conflict to emerge?
     (b) Process assessment: Did TechFlow's escalation and resolution
         procedures function as designed, or are revisions needed?
     (c) Relationship impact: What steps are required to restore or
         reinforce trust with the affected stakeholder?
     (d) Policy update trigger: Does this conflict reveal a gap in existing
         TechFlow policy that requires formal amendment?
   Completed reviews are submitted to the Compliance function and inform
   the annual update of TechFlow's Account Management Ethics Guidelines.
   The goal is not only to resolve individual conflicts but to continuously
   build an organizational culture where ethical account management is the
   professional standard, not the exception.
