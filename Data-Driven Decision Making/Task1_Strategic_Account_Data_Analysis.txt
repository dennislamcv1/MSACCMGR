----------------------------------------------------------------
DELIVERABLE 1: PORTFOLIO ANALYSIS SUMMARY (120–150 words)
----------------------------------------------------------------

Over the past 18 months, analysis of the six-account portfolio
reveals a bifurcated performance pattern: three accounts show
strong, consistent growth in platform usage and feature adoption,
while the remaining three exhibit declining engagement, reduced
support ticket activity, and slower license utilization — early
churn signals that warrant immediate attention.

Using Excel pivot tables, month-over-month ARR trends and seat
utilization rates were isolated per account, exposing a 14%
aggregate usage decline in the bottom tier. Power BI dashboards
visualized engagement velocity across product modules, highlighting
that accounts with executive sponsor disengagement correlate
directly with reduced renewal confidence scores. Copilot-generated
summaries accelerated pattern recognition across large engagement
log datasets, surfacing that two mid-tier accounts had quietly
reduced active users by 22% without triggering standard alerts.

These findings confirm the need for differentiated engagement
strategies calibrated to each account's health trajectory.

DELIVERABLE 2: ACCOUNT SEGMENTATION OUTLINE (4–6 Items)
----------------------------------------------------------------

SEGMENT 1 — STRATEGIC GROWTH CHAMPIONS
  Defining Data Characteristics:
    • ARR growth rate above 18% year-over-year
    • Platform utilization consistently above 85%
    • High feature adoption across core and advanced modules
    • Active executive sponsor engagement (QBR attendance rate 100%)
    • Net Promoter Score (NPS) of 8–10
  Strategic Implications:
    Prioritize executive co-innovation sessions and early access to
    new product features. Develop formal case study and reference
    partnerships. Assign top-tier CSM resources to protect and
    expand. Target upsell opportunities in adjacent product lines
    with a focus on multi-year contract conversion.

SEGMENT 2 — STABLE PERFORMERS
  Defining Data Characteristics:
    • ARR growth rate between 5–17% year-over-year
    • Utilization rates ranging from 65–84%
    • Moderate feature adoption; core modules active, advanced
      features underutilized
    • Consistent renewal history with minimal escalation activity
    • NPS of 6–7
  Strategic Implications:
    Focus on deepening product adoption through targeted enablement
    and training campaigns. Identify specific workflow gaps where
    advanced features would add measurable value. Conduct annual
    Business Value Reviews (BVRs) to reinforce ROI and quantify
    outcomes. These accounts represent the most accessible
    expansion pipeline with relatively low risk.

SEGMENT 3 — ENGAGEMENT-DECLINING ACCOUNTS (At-Risk)
  Defining Data Characteristics:
    • Active user count declined 15–25% over the past two quarters
    • Support ticket volume dropped sharply — indicating
      disengagement rather than satisfaction
    • Executive sponsor contact frequency reduced significantly
    • License utilization below 60%
    • Renewal date within 6–9 months
  Strategic Implications:
    Trigger an immediate account health review and executive
    outreach. Deploy a structured re-engagement plan including
    health check workshops, personalized adoption roadmaps, and
    ROI reaffirmation sessions. Escalate internally to leadership
    for executive-to-executive relationship rebuilding. Assign
    dedicated risk mitigation resources and establish 30-60-90 day
    milestone check-ins to track recovery progress.

SEGMENT 4 — EXPANSION-READY MID-MARKET ACCOUNTS
  Defining Data Characteristics:
    • Current ARR in mid-range tier with documented budget growth
    • High utilization of existing licensed seats (90%+ capacity)
    • Multiple internal champions identified across departments
    • Frequent feature requests and product feedback submissions
    • Organic usage of product beyond initial defined scope
  Strategic Implications:
    Present formal expansion proposals targeting additional seat
    licenses, module upgrades, or platform tier advancement.
    Leverage multi-champion presence to reduce single point-of-
    contact risk. Align expansion conversations with the client's
    fiscal planning cycle to maximize budget capture. Use usage
    data to build a compelling, data-backed business case.

SEGMENT 5 — TRANSACTIONAL LOW-ENGAGEMENT ACCOUNTS
  Defining Data Characteristics:
    • Minimal outbound engagement outside of renewal touchpoints
    • Below-average feature adoption; primarily using entry-level
      product functionality
    • No executive sponsor identified or actively engaged
    • Flat ARR with no growth trajectory over 18 months
    • Support interactions limited to basic troubleshooting
  Strategic Implications:
    Evaluate the cost-to-serve ratio relative to ARR contribution.
    Implement a low-touch digital engagement model (automated
    health checks, self-service resources, targeted email campaigns)
    to maintain the relationship efficiently. Assign a scaled CSM
    coverage model rather than dedicated resources. If no growth
    signal emerges within two quarters, develop a structured
    conversation around right-sizing the contract or identifying a
    new internal champion to revitalize strategic relevance.

DELIVERABLE 3: RISK AND OPPORTUNITY INSIGHTS (3–5 Items)
----------------------------------------------------------------

INSIGHT 1 — RISK: Silent Disengagement at Accounts 4 and 6
  Data Observation:
    Power BI engagement dashboards revealed that active user counts
    at two accounts dropped by 22% over the trailing 90 days, yet
    no formal support escalations or complaints were filed. This
    "silent churn" pattern — where declining usage goes unreported —
    is a well-documented leading indicator of non-renewal.
  Recommended Action:
    Schedule immediate executive sponsor outreach within 10 business
    days. Conduct a usage audit workshop to identify workflow
    blockers, undiscovered features, or organizational changes
    driving disengagement. Develop a 60-day re-onboarding plan with
    defined success milestones and bi-weekly health check calls.

INSIGHT 2 — OPPORTUNITY: Seat Expansion at Account 2
  Data Observation:
    Account 2 is currently operating at 94% licensed seat capacity,
    with usage logs showing frequent activity from users flagged as
    non-licensed collaborators accessing shared outputs. This organic
    over-usage signals unmet demand and strong product stickiness
    beyond the contracted user base.
  Recommended Action:
    Present a formal seat expansion proposal in the next scheduled
    touchpoint. Use actual usage data to frame the conversation as
    a natural next step aligned to their operational growth. Offer
    a multi-year pricing incentive to close before the current
    contract anniversary date.

INSIGHT 3 — RISK: Single Executive Sponsor Dependency at Account 3
  Data Observation:
    QBR attendance records and CRM contact logs show that 100% of
    strategic engagement at Account 3 flows through a single VP-level
    contact. LinkedIn and public company news monitoring (surfaced via
    Copilot) flagged this individual's name in recent organizational
    restructuring announcements, elevating relationship continuity risk.
  Recommended Action:
    Immediately initiate a multi-threading strategy by requesting
    introductions to additional stakeholders (IT, Finance, and
    Operations leads) through the existing sponsor. Conduct a
    departmental value mapping exercise to establish independent
    relationships across the organization before any leadership
    transition occurs.

INSIGHT 4 — OPPORTUNITY: Advanced Module Upsell at Accounts 1 and 5
  Data Observation:
    Excel usage frequency analysis shows that both Account 1 and
    Account 5 are generating the maximum output volume possible
    within their current module tier, with Copilot analysis
    identifying repeated workflow patterns that the advanced
    analytics and automation module is specifically designed to
    address. Neither account has been formally presented with
    an upgrade path.
  Recommended Action:
    Build a tailored ROI model for each account demonstrating
    measurable time and cost savings from upgrading to the advanced
    module. Present findings during the next business review in a
    co-value workshop format. Engage product specialists to run a
    30-day pilot at no cost to reduce adoption friction and
    accelerate the decision cycle.

INSIGHT 5 — RISK: Renewal Concentration Risk in Q3
  Data Observation:
    Renewal timeline analysis in Excel reveals that three of the six
    portfolio accounts — representing 61% of total portfolio ARR —
    have contract renewals falling within the same 45-day window in
    Q3. This concentration creates significant revenue volatility
    risk if any one account faces delays or non-renewal decisions
    during a compressed period.
  Recommended Action:
    Begin formal renewal conversations with all three accounts a
    minimum of 120 days in advance of their renewal dates. Where
    feasible, negotiate staggered multi-year agreements to distribute
    renewal risk across fiscal quarters. Flag the concentration
    exposure to leadership as part of the quarterly portfolio review
    to ensure adequate cross-functional support is allocated during
    the critical renewal window.
