DELIVERABLE 1: MEDDIC-BASED OPPORTUNITY QUALIFICATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1. METRICS -- Quantifying the Value GlobalCorp Expects to Achieve
   GlobalCorp's digital transformation initiative is driven by measurable
   operational improvement targets across its 14 international sites.
   Key metrics anchoring this engagement include:
     - 22% reduction in manual operational processing time across core
       business units within 18 months of platform deployment
     - $4.7M in annual cost avoidance from legacy infrastructure
       decommissioning over the five-year partnership term
     - 35% improvement in cross-regional data reporting cycle time,
       reducing month-end close from 12 days to under 8 days
     - Target platform adoption rate of 85% of active users within
       12 months of each regional rollout phase

   Strategic Justification: Attaching TechFlow's value proposition to
   GlobalCorp's own declared performance targets -- rather than generic
   industry benchmarks -- transforms the business case from aspirational
   to accountable. Metrics defined early in the engagement become the
   shared language of success and reduce post-sale scope disputes.

--------

2. ECONOMIC BUYER -- Identifying Who Controls the Budget Decision
   Primary Economic Buyer: Marcus Lindqvist, Chief Financial Officer
   Secondary Economic Buyer: Dr. Sandra Osei, Chief Information Officer
   (co-approval authority on technology investments above $2M)

   The CFO holds capital expenditure authority for this investment and
   will evaluate TechFlow's proposal through the lens of total cost of
   ownership, payback period, and balance sheet treatment (CAPEX vs.
   OPEX structuring). The CIO controls the technology transformation
   budget envelope and influences whether the investment is positioned
   as infrastructure or strategic capability.

   Strategic Justification: Failing to engage the true economic buyer
   early -- and instead focusing exclusively on operational or technical
   stakeholders -- is one of the most common causes of late-stage deal
   failure. Both the CFO and CIO must receive tailored financial narratives
   that speak to their distinct evaluation frameworks before any commercial
   proposal is formally submitted.

--------

3. DECISION CRITERIA -- What GlobalCorp Will Use to Select a Vendor
   GlobalCorp's evaluation framework, as identified through discovery
   conversations, encompasses five weighted criteria:
     (a) Platform scalability and enterprise architecture fit -- 30%
     (b) Security certifications and regulatory compliance posture -- 25%
     (c) Implementation methodology and phased deployment capability -- 20%
     (d) Total cost of ownership and commercial flexibility -- 15%
     (e) Vendor financial stability and long-term partnership commitment -- 10%

   Strategic Justification: Understanding decision criteria before the
   formal RFP stage allows TechFlow to shape proposal structure, reference
   customer selection, and demonstration sequencing around the factors that
   matter most to GlobalCorp's evaluators. We actively confirm and update
   these weightings through ongoing stakeholder dialogue rather than
   assuming they are static across the evaluation lifecycle.

--------

4. DECISION PROCESS -- How GlobalCorp Will Reach a Final Decision
   Based on procurement team engagement, GlobalCorp's decision process
   follows a structured six-stage sequence:
     Stage 1: Internal needs validation and budget approval (complete)
     Stage 2: Long-list vendor RFI -- responses due within 21 days
     Stage 3: Short-list selection (target: 3 vendors) and RFP issuance
     Stage 4: Vendor presentations and platform demonstrations
     Stage 5: Reference checks, security assessments, and site visits
     Stage 6: Commercial negotiation and contract execution
              (target completion: end of Q3 of current fiscal year)

   Key internal approvers required for final sign-off: CFO, CIO, General
   Counsel, and Head of Procurement. Board-level notification (not approval)
   required for contracts exceeding $5M.

   Strategic Justification: Mapping the decision process prevents TechFlow
   from accelerating commercially before GlobalCorp's internal governance is
   ready, which creates pressure that damages relationships. It also enables
   proactive planning -- ensuring reference customers, security
   documentation, and legal resources are positioned at the right stage.

--------

5. IDENTIFIED PAIN -- The Business Problems Driving Urgency
   Through structured discovery, four primary pain points have been confirmed
   across GlobalCorp's business units:

     (a) OPERATIONAL FRAGMENTATION: GlobalCorp operates across 14 sites
         using seven distinct legacy systems that do not share data in real
         time, resulting in significant manual reconciliation effort and
         reporting inaccuracies that affect quarterly decision-making.

     (b) REGULATORY EXPOSURE: GlobalCorp's current data architecture
         does not fully meet GDPR Article 25 (data protection by design)
         requirements, creating audit risk ahead of a scheduled regulatory
         review in the next 12 months.

     (c) TALENT AND PRODUCTIVITY LOSS: Line managers report that knowledge
         workers spend an estimated 6.5 hours per week on manual data
         consolidation tasks that a unified platform would automate,
         representing approximately $2.1M in annual productivity cost
         across targeted business units.

     (d) COMPETITIVE DISADVANTAGE: Two primary GlobalCorp competitors have
         completed or are nearing completion of comparable transformation
         programs, creating pressure on GlobalCorp's executive team from
         the board to accelerate its own digital capability roadmap.

   Strategic Justification: Pain drives urgency. Quantified, confirmed pain
   points -- particularly those with regulatory deadlines or board visibility
   -- convert a "nice to have" conversation into a "must act" mandate.
   TechFlow's solution narrative must be anchored to these specific pain
   points, not presented as a generic platform pitch.

--------

6. CHAMPION -- Who Will Advocate for TechFlow Internally
   Primary Champion: Dr. Sandra Osei, Chief Information Officer
   Supporting Champion: Hiroshi Tanaka, APAC Regional Business Unit Lead

   The CIO has publicly endorsed digital transformation as her defining
   leadership priority for the next two fiscal years and has spoken
   positively about TechFlow's architecture approach in discovery sessions.
   She has the organizational credibility to carry the internal business
   case and the executive access to influence both the CFO and board
   communications. Hiroshi Tanaka is an emerging champion at the
   operational level whose early adoption advocacy in APAC will generate
   visible proof-of-value that strengthens the CIO's internal narrative.

   Strategic Justification: A champion without organizational influence
   is simply an enthusiast. TechFlow must invest deliberately in the CIO's
   success -- equipping her with data, talking points, and executive
   materials she can use to build internal consensus independently of
   TechFlow's presence in the room. Champion development is an active,
   ongoing account management responsibility, not a one-time event.

DELIVERABLE 2: CONSULTATIVE ENGAGEMENT STRATEGY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

TechFlow's engagement approach positions the account team as a strategic
advisor to GlobalCorp's leadership -- not a vendor seeking transaction approval.
Advisory credibility is built through three interlocking practices: deep
preparation, insight-led conversation, and disciplined follow-through.

Before each stakeholder engagement, the account team synthesizes CRM data --
including interaction history, stated priorities, open concerns, and
deal-stage signals -- with externally validated insights such as GlobalCorp's
public earnings commentary, regulatory filings, and industry benchmarking data.
This preparation enables conversations that reflect genuine understanding of
GlobalCorp's business context, not generic sales patter.

Stakeholder engagement is sequenced deliberately: technical credibility is
established with operational and IT leaders first, followed by business-case
validation with Finance, and relationship-level executive engagement with the
CIO and CFO. CRM pipeline data is reviewed weekly to identify engagement gaps,
stakeholder sentiment shifts, and risk signals requiring proactive response.

All Copilot-generated research summaries and talking point drafts are reviewed
and validated by the account team before use, ensuring that insight quality
reflects human judgment alongside AI efficiency. The result is an engagement
model that GlobalCorp stakeholders experience as informed, consistent, and
genuinely oriented toward their outcomes.

DELIVERABLE 3: VALUE PROPOSITION & BUSINESS CASE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1. OPERATIONAL EFFICIENCY -- $2.1M ANNUAL PRODUCTIVITY RECOVERY
   TechFlow's unified data platform eliminates the manual reconciliation
   burden currently consuming an estimated 6.5 hours per knowledge worker
   per week across GlobalCorp's targeted business units. Automation of
   data consolidation and cross-system reporting workflows is projected to
   recover $2.1M in annual productivity value within 12 months of full
   platform adoption.
   Business Impact: Redirects high-cost knowledge worker capacity toward
   strategic analysis and decision support, improving organizational
   responsiveness and reducing burnout-driven attrition in affected teams.
   This ROI alone offsets more than 65% of the annual recurring platform fee.

--------

2. LEGACY INFRASTRUCTURE DECOMMISSIONING -- $4.7M COST AVOIDANCE OVER 5 YEARS
   GlobalCorp currently maintains seven fragmented legacy systems across
   its 14 international sites, each carrying ongoing licensing, support,
   and integration maintenance costs. TechFlow's platform consolidates these
   into a single enterprise architecture, enabling the phased decommissioning
   of redundant systems and avoiding an estimated $4.7M in cumulative
   infrastructure costs over the five-year partnership term.
   Business Impact: Converts a distributed, unmanaged technology cost base
   into a predictable, consolidated investment with a single vendor
   accountability model -- directly addressing the CFO's total cost of
   ownership concerns and improving balance sheet planning visibility.

--------

3. REGULATORY RISK MITIGATION -- ESTIMATED $1.8M PENALTY EXPOSURE REDUCED
   GlobalCorp's current data architecture presents material GDPR compliance
   gaps ahead of a scheduled regulatory review within the next 12 months.
   Documented enforcement data from comparable GDPR violations in GlobalCorp's
   industry sector suggests potential penalty exposure in the range of
   $1.5M--$2.1M. TechFlow's platform is architected to meet GDPR Article 25
   (data protection by design) requirements and includes built-in data
   residency controls, audit trail generation, and consent management
   capabilities.
   Business Impact: Positions GlobalCorp to enter its regulatory review
   with a demonstrably improved compliance posture, reducing penalty
   exposure and avoiding the reputational and operational disruption of
   a material regulatory finding during a critical transformation period.

--------

4. REPORTING CYCLE ACCELERATION -- 35% FASTER MONTH-END CLOSE
   TechFlow's real-time cross-regional data consolidation capabilities
   address GlobalCorp's current 12-day month-end reporting cycle,
   which Finance leadership has identified as a strategic limitation on
   executive decision speed. Comparable deployments in similar enterprise
   environments have reduced month-end close cycles by 33%--38%, supporting
   a target of under 8 business days for GlobalCorp.
   Business Impact: Accelerates the availability of financial and
   operational intelligence for the CFO and business unit leads, enabling
   faster course-correction on underperforming regions, more responsive
   investor communications, and improved board-level confidence in
   management reporting quality.

--------

5. COMPETITIVE POSITIONING -- CAPABILITY PARITY WITH LEADING SECTOR PEERS
   Two of GlobalCorp's primary competitors have completed or are actively
   completing comparable enterprise platform transformations. GlobalCorp's
   board has flagged digital capability gap as a strategic risk in the
   most recent annual strategic review. TechFlow's partnership accelerates
   GlobalCorp's path to digital capability parity -- and in targeted
   capability areas (AI-assisted operations and cross-border data management)
   -- to competitive differentiation.
   Business Impact: Enables GlobalCorp's executive team to reframe the
   digital transformation narrative internally and externally: from
   "catching up to competitors" to "building the platform for next-decade
   growth." This positioning supports talent attraction, investor confidence,
   and strategic M&A readiness in markets where technology capability is
   increasingly a deal-qualifying criterion.
