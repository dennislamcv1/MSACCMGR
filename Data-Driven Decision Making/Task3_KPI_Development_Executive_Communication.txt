DELIVERABLE 1: STRATEGIC KPI FRAMEWORK (4–6 Items)
----------------------------------------------------------------

KPI 1 — NET REVENUE RETENTION (NRR)
  Definition:
    The percentage of recurring revenue retained from existing
    accounts over a defined period, inclusive of expansions,
    contractions, and churn. Calculated as: (Beginning ARR +
    Expansion ARR − Contraction ARR − Churned ARR) ÷ Beginning
    ARR × 100.
  Target Benchmark: ≥ 110% (indicating portfolio-level growth
    from existing accounts alone)
  Why It Matters:
    NRR is the single most comprehensive indicator of portfolio
    financial health. A reading above 100% confirms that expansion
    revenue is outpacing losses — the defining characteristic of
    a self-growing account base. For this portfolio, NRR surfaces
    whether the risk-weighted investment strategy in Task 2 is
    producing measurable retention and expansion outcomes, and it
    is the metric most closely scrutinized by executive leadership
    and board-level stakeholders when assessing go-to-market
    efficiency and customer success performance.

KPI 2 — PLATFORM UTILIZATION RATE
  Definition:
    The percentage of licensed product capacity actively used by
    each account during a rolling 30-day period. Measured as active
    users ÷ total licensed seats × 100, with additional tracking
    of module-level feature adoption depth.
  Target Benchmark: ≥ 80% across the portfolio; accounts below
    60% flagged as high-risk.
  Why It Matters:
    Utilization rate is the earliest leading indicator of churn
    risk and expansion readiness — often preceding formal churn
    signals by 60–90 days. Accounts operating above 90% capacity
    present a natural, data-supported entry point for expansion
    conversations. Accounts trending downward signal adoption
    friction, organizational changes, or competitive displacement
    that require immediate intervention. This KPI directly informed
    the at-risk identification in Task 1 and underpins the
    re-engagement strategy for accounts 4 and 6.

KPI 3 — EXECUTIVE ENGAGEMENT INDEX (EEI)
  Definition:
    A composite score measuring the frequency, recency, and
    seniority of executive-level interactions per account over a
    rolling 90-day window. Inputs include QBR attendance, executive
    sponsor meeting frequency, C-suite email response rates, and
    escalation involvement. Scored on a 1–10 scale.
  Target Benchmark: ≥ 7.0 per account; accounts scoring below
    5.0 flagged for immediate multi-threading outreach.
  Why It Matters:
    Research consistently shows that accounts with active, senior-
    level engagement renew at significantly higher rates and expand
    more frequently than those managed solely at the operational
    level. The EEI quantifies relationship depth in a way that CRM
    contact counts alone cannot, capturing whether DataTech has
    meaningful access to decision-makers — not just practitioners.
    For Account 3, where single-sponsor dependency was identified
    as a critical risk, the EEI provides an objective metric to
    track multi-threading progress over time.

KPI 4 — RENEWAL PROBABILITY SCORE (RPS)
  Definition:
    A predictive, AI-assisted score assigned to each account 120–
    180 days prior to renewal, synthesizing utilization trends,
    engagement index, support escalation frequency, NPS, and
    historical renewal behavior into a weighted probability
    percentage. Modeled using Copilot-integrated forecasting logic
    applied to CRM and product usage data.
  Target Benchmark: RPS ≥ 75% for all accounts entering the
    renewal window; accounts below 60% escalated to leadership.
  Why It Matters:
    The RPS converts qualitative relationship judgment into a
    quantifiable, action-triggering metric that enables leadership
    to prioritize renewal resources objectively and early. Rather
    than relying on CSM instinct or last-minute deal reviews, the
    RPS creates a structured, data-driven renewal pipeline visible
    to leadership 4–6 months in advance. Given that three accounts
    renew within a compressed 45-day Q3 window, having RPS scores
    updated monthly for each allows for differentiated intervention
    timelines rather than reactive, simultaneous crisis management.

KPI 5 — CUSTOMER HEALTH SCORE (CHS)
  Definition:
    A weighted composite score (1–100) aggregating platform
    utilization rate (30%), Executive Engagement Index (25%),
    support ticket sentiment and resolution time (20%), NPS or
    CSAT survey results (15%), and product adoption breadth (10%).
    Updated monthly per account and displayed as a color-coded
    status indicator: Green (75–100), Yellow (50–74), Red (0–49).
  Target Benchmark: Portfolio average CHS ≥ 75; no more than
    one account in Red status at any given time.
  Why It Matters:
    The CHS serves as the portfolio's universal health language —
    a single, interpretable number that synthesizes multi-source
    account data into an executive-ready signal. It enables
    leadership to assess portfolio risk in seconds without requiring
    deep familiarity with each account's individual dynamics. For
    the CSM team, it creates a shared, objective accountability
    framework. For this portfolio specifically, the CHS would
    have surfaced the silent disengagement at accounts 4 and 6
    approximately two months earlier than the manual review
    conducted in Task 1.

KPI 6 — EXPANSION ARR PIPELINE VALUE
  Definition:
    The total value of identified, qualified upsell and cross-sell
    opportunities across the portfolio at any given point in time,
    expressed in projected incremental ARR. Opportunities are
    qualified based on product fit, budget confirmation signal,
    champion identification, and a defined decision timeline within
    the next two quarters.
  Target Benchmark: Expansion pipeline value ≥ 25% of current
    portfolio ARR ($1.05M target against the $4.2M base).
  Why It Matters:
    Tracking expansion pipeline as a formal KPI — rather than an
    informal CSM estimate — creates executive visibility into
    organic growth potential and ensures that upsell opportunities
    are actively developed, not discovered accidentally at renewal.
    It also enables resource planning decisions: knowing that
    $380,000 in qualified expansion ARR sits at two accounts
    justifies the investment in co-innovation sessions and advanced
    module pilots outlined in the financial strategy. This KPI
    closes the loop between the account health analysis and the
    revenue growth forecast.

DELIVERABLE 2: DASHBOARD & VISUALIZATION DESCRIPTION (120–150 words)
----------------------------------------------------------------

The Power BI portfolio dashboard is structured as a three-panel
executive view designed for rapid interpretation at the leadership
level. The top panel features a Portfolio Health Scorecard — a
color-coded tile matrix displaying each account's Customer Health
Score, Renewal Probability Score, and NRR status in a single
glance, enabling instant risk triage without navigating individual
account records.

The center panel presents a dual-axis trend line chart overlaying
platform utilization rates against ARR growth trajectory for all
six accounts over the trailing 18 months, making engagement-to-
revenue correlation immediately visible. A scatter plot within the
same panel maps accounts by expansion pipeline value against
renewal probability, allowing leadership to prioritize both
protection and growth investments simultaneously.

The bottom panel features a waterfall chart illustrating projected
NRR contribution by account — separating baseline retention,
expansion uplift, and churn-risk exposure — alongside a quarterly
renewal timeline bar chart flagging Q3 concentration risk.

Copilot was used during dashboard design to generate natural-
language narrative summaries embedded directly beneath each
visualization panel, translating data patterns into plain-language
insights that non-technical stakeholders can interpret and act on
without requiring analytical support. This design choice reduced
executive preparation time for quarterly business reviews by
removing the need for a separate written briefing document.

DELIVERABLE 3: EXECUTIVE INSIGHT SUMMARY (100–120 words)
----------------------------------------------------------------

The portfolio is performing with meaningful internal divergence.
Three accounts — representing approximately $2.1M in ARR — are
demonstrating strong health scores, active engagement, and
expansion readiness that positions them for 15–20% revenue growth
within the next 18 months. The remaining three accounts carry
measurable churn risk, concentrated renewal exposure in Q3, and
declining utilization trends that require immediate, structured
intervention.

The recommended course of action is to authorize the Risk-Weighted
Investment Strategy, directing differentiated resources toward both
recovery and expansion simultaneously. Doing so is projected to
raise portfolio NRR from an estimated 93% to 108–112%, protect
approximately $840,000 in at-risk renewal ARR, and unlock a
qualified expansion pipeline valued at $310,000–$380,000 in net
new ARR.

Inaction carries a compounding cost: accounts currently trending
toward disengagement will not self-correct. The data is clear —
the window for proactive intervention is now, and the financial
case for acting decisively is stronger than the cost of delay.
