PRESENTER PRINCIPLES FOR EXECUTIVE AUDIENCE
  1. LEAD WITH OUTCOMES — Always frame capability statements in terms of
     what HS Regional gains, not what CloudTech does.
  2. NAME THE NUMBERS — Executives respond to specific, referenced data
     points. Avoid vague claims ("we deliver high quality"). Use evidence.
  3. ACKNOWLEDGE COMPLEXITY — Credibility is built by naming the hard parts
     (EHR integration risk, clinical adoption challenges) before the client
     raises them. Proactive candor is a differentiator.
  4. CREATE SILENCE — The most powerful moments in an executive presentation
     are the pauses after a key data point or strategic statement. Resist
     the urge to fill every second with words.
  5. CLOSE EVERY SECTION WITH A PARTNERSHIP STATEMENT — Consistently connect
     each capability back to the long-term relationship vision. The $15M
     program must be subtly present throughout, not just in Section 6.
